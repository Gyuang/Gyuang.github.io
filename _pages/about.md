## Hello, I'm Sungkyu Yang

I'm an integrated **MS/PhD student** at the **[AI Graduate School](https://ai.gist.ac.kr/), Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology (GIST)**, advised by **Prof. Mansu Kim**. My research sits at the intersection of **multimodal alignment**, **language models**, and **spatial transcriptomics** — building AI systems that read across images, text, and biology to support clinical and biomedical discovery.

Before AI, I was a mechanical and robotics engineer. I came to ML through autonomous driving competitions and a growing conviction that the hardest real-world problems — clinical decision-making, biological systems — need models that can align signals across very different modalities. That's what I work on now.

## Research Interests

- **Multimodal Alignment** — learning shared representations across images, text, and structured biological data.
- **Language Models** — adapting and evaluating LLMs for scientific and clinical reasoning (prompt-based and retrieval-augmented).
- **Spatial Transcriptomics** — predicting gene expression from histology; bridging pathology and molecular biology with deep learning.

*Adjacent interests: vision-language models for medical imaging, parameter-efficient adaptation (prompt tuning, adapters), uncertainty quantification for trustworthy clinical AI.*

## Current Projects

At AIMED-Lab I'm actively working on:

- **Leptomeningeal Metastasis Classification** — AI models for detecting and characterizing leptomeningeal metastasis from medical imaging.
- **Whole-Slide Image (WSI) Segmentation** — tissue- and cell-level segmentation on gigapixel pathology slides.
- **CT Report Generation** — multimodal LLM pipelines that generate radiology reports grounded in 3D CT volumes.
- **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) for Clinical Reasoning** — grounding LLM diagnoses in medical literature and structured knowledge.
- **Spatial Transcriptomics — Gene Expression Prediction** — predicting spatial gene expression directly from histology images.
