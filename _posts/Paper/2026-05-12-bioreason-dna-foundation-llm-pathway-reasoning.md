## TL;DR

- BioReason linearly projects frozen DNA-foundation-model embeddings (Evo2-1B or Nucleotide-Transformer-500M) into the token stream of a fine-tuned Qwen3 LLM, bracketed by `<dna_start>` / `<dna_end>` tokens, so the LLM can attend over per-nucleotide features and emit a `<think>` chain-of-thought before predicting a disease label.
- Two-stage training: supervised fine-tuning on Claude-3.7-generated reasoning traces, then Dr.GRPO reinforcement learning with a composite reward (+2.0 correctness, +0.5 conciseness ≤10 words, +0.5 single `<think>` block; max 2.5).
- Headline result on the curated 290-sample KEGG pathway test set: **86.55% (NT-only) / 88.28% (Evo2-only) → 98.28% accuracy and 93.05% macro-F1** for Evo2-1B + Qwen3-4B + GRPO; on the chromosome-8 held-out VEP-Coding task Evo2+Qwen3-4B reaches **80.21% accuracy / 80.00% F1 vs. 48.99 / 39.58 for Qwen3-4B alone** — a +31-point lift that cannot be explained by lexical gene-symbol leakage.

## Motivation

Genomic foundation models (Evo2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, DNABERT-2) compress long DNA context into rich per-token embeddings but only expose discriminative scores — there is no mechanism for them to *explain* why a variant is pathogenic. LLMs, conversely, treat ACGT as raw text and miss long-range regulatory grammar that the genomic FMs already encode. For variant-effect interpretation and pathway-level disease reasoning — both core medical-genomics workflows that today rely on manual literature review — neither modality alone suffices. BioReason is positioned as the first system that deeply binds a DNA-FM to an LLM so that mechanism-level chain-of-thought is generated *over* genomic embeddings, not as a post-hoc paraphrase of a classifier output.

## Core Innovation

- **Token-stream fusion of a frozen DNA-FM with an LLM.** A single learnable linear projector `Proj: R^{d_dna} -> R^{d_llm}` maps Evo2 / NT per-token embeddings into Qwen3's embedding space. The DNA encoder is never updated; only the projector and LoRA adapters on the LLM are trained.
- **Reasoning supervision, not just classification.** SFT targets are full `<think>...</think>` traces (mean 303.8 words) generated by Claude 3.7 Sonnet from KEGG disease descriptions — the model is explicitly trained to imitate multi-step pathway reasoning.
- **Dr.GRPO RL with a compact composite reward.** Group-relative PPO without standard-deviation normalization, 8 samples per query, reward in [0, 2.5]. The conciseness and single-`<think>`-block sub-rewards are anti-loophole defenses against the usual RLHF reward-hacking failure modes.

**Honest read.** The 98.28% KEGG headline is arithmetically correct but evidentially fragile, for three compounding reasons.

1. **The DNA-FM contribution is never isolated from gene-symbol leakage.** The KEGG prompt (Fig. 2B) names the affected gene, enumerates every gene in the pathway with descriptions, and encodes topology symbolically (`GENE1 -> GENE2 -| GENE3`). Qwen3-4B with text-only input *already* reaches 90.00% accuracy / 79.66% F1 — so on KEGG the DNA-FM contributes at most a +8.28 pt accuracy / +13.39 pt F1 marginal lift, not the full 86→98 jump. There is no ablation that (a) strips gene names from the prompt, (b) replaces real DNA with shuffled / random nucleotides, or (c) compares against a "gene-symbol + pathway only, no DNA" baseline. Without these, "Evo2 encodes genomic signal" cannot be distinguished from "Evo2 acts as a soft-prompt-like extra capacity."

2. **Contamination cannot be ruled out on KEGG.** The reasoning-trace supervision was written by Claude 3.7 Sonnet, which has very likely seen KEGG; gene-disease associations like LRRK2→Parkinson's or PFN1→ALS are textbook facts memorized by Qwen3's pretraining corpus; and the train/test split is by variant network, so the same gene families reappear at test with different point mutations. The test set is 290 examples — one wrong answer is 0.34 pt, and the NeurIPS checklist concedes no multi-seed runs were performed.

3. **The cleanest result is buried.** On the chromosome-8 held-out VEP-Coding task the LLM-only baseline genuinely fails (Qwen3-4B 48.99 Acc / 39.58 F1) on the same gene-symbol-containing prompt, so the +31-point lift to Evo2+Qwen3-4B 80.21 / 80.00 cannot be attributed to lexical leakage. This is the strongest evidence that the DNA-FM is doing real genomic work — and it should arguably have been the headline rather than 86→98.

## Method & Architecture

![BioReason architecture diagram](/assets/images/paper/bioreason/page_004.png)
*Figure 1: BioReason architecture. A frozen DNA foundation model (Evo2-1B or NT-500M) encodes paired reference/variant sequences; a single learnable linear projector maps the per-token embeddings into Qwen3's input space between `<dna_start>` and `<dna_end>` tokens; the LLM emits a `<think>` chain-of-thought followed by the disease prediction. Only the projector and LLM LoRA adapters are trained.*

### 1. Inputs

A pair of DNA sequences `S_DNA` (reference + variant, mean ≈4 kbp, truncated/tokenized to ≤2048 DNA tokens) plus a natural-language query `Q_TEXT` carrying chromosome, gene list, pathway notation, and gene descriptions.

### 2. DNA encoder (frozen)

Either StripedHyena2-based **Evo2-1B** or **Nucleotide-Transformer-500M** produces `E_DNA in R^{L' x d_dna}`. Weights are never updated — Evo2's pretraining cost is recovered as a fixed feature extractor.

### 3. Projection and assembly

A learnable linear layer maps `d_dna -> d_llm`. The unified input becomes
`X_LLM = (e_<dna_start>, E'_DNA, e_<dna_end>, E_Qtext)`,
with RoPE applied across the joined sequence.

### 4. LLM backbone

Qwen3-1.7B or Qwen3-4B with LoRA (rank 32 / α 64 / dropout 0.05 for SFT; rank 16 / α 32 for GRPO). AdamW, lr 5e-5 (SFT) / 1e-5 (GRPO), weight decay 1e-2.

### 5. Stage 1 — Supervised fine-tuning

Standard next-token NLL,

$$\mathcal{L} = \sum_i \log P(y_i \mid y_{<i}, X_{LLM}; \theta_{LLM}),$$

with a custom attention mask restricting the loss to tokens after `<think>` — DNA embeddings and the textual query are not loss targets.

### 6. Stage 2 — Dr.GRPO reinforcement learning

Sample G=8 generations per query, score with the composite reward, compute advantages `A_i = r_i − mean(r)` (Dr.GRPO drops std normalization to remove length/difficulty bias), and optimize a clipped PPO surrogate with ε=0.2, KL weight β=0.0. The reward (range [0, 2.5]) is:

- +2.0 if the post-`</think>` answer contains the ground-truth disease string (case-insensitive substring match).
- +0.5 if the final answer is ≤10 words.
- +0.5 if exactly one `<think>...</think>` block precedes the final answer.

### 7. Decoding

Temperature 0, vLLM serving, deterministic single-run evaluation.

The KEGG split is by *variant*, not by disease or gene, so well-known disease genes (LRRK2, PFN1, APP, SOD1, ...) recur across train and test. The disease distribution is heavily neurological-dominated; Table 4 reports near-100% accuracy on the 10 highest-frequency classes (Parkinson's n=47, Alzheimer's n=40, spinocerebellar ataxia n=36, ALS n=35, Huntington's, prion, Gaucher, AML, melanoma, colorectal cancer) but the remaining 27 long-tail diseases are not broken out.

## Experimental Results

### KEGG-derived pathway reasoning (290 test points)

| Model | Acc. | F1 | Prec. | Rec. |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| NT-500M (DNA-only) | 86.55 | 69.76 | 73.23 | 66.61 |
| Evo2-1B (DNA-only) | 88.28 | 72.43 | 75.23 | 69.83 |
| Qwen3-1B (LLM-only) | 85.17 | 65.71 | 71.39 | 64.19 |
| Qwen3-4B (LLM-only) | 90.00 | 79.66 | 88.24 | 75.08 |
| NT + Qwen3-1B | 89.31 | 81.46 | 88.24 | 77.30 |
| NT + Qwen3-1B (+GRPO) | 91.72 | 75.06 | 79.41 | 72.89 |
| NT + Qwen3-4B | 95.86 | 86.25 | 88.24 | 84.95 |
| **NT + Qwen3-4B (+GRPO)** | **98.28** | 90.15 | 91.18 | 89.62 |
| Evo2 + Qwen3-1B | 90.42 | 75.62 | 77.42 | 73.91 |
| Evo2 + Qwen3-4B | 95.17 | 86.14 | 91.18 | 83.33 |
| **Evo2 + Qwen3-4B (+GRPO)** | **98.28** | **93.05** | **94.12** | **92.48** |

Note that Qwen3-4B alone — operating on the *same* gene-symbol-rich prompt with no DNA channel — already reaches 90.00% accuracy. The DNA-FM marginal contribution on KEGG is therefore at most ≈+8 pt accuracy, not the full +12 pt advertised against the 86.55% NT-only baseline.

### Variant-Effect Prediction (Table 2)

| Model | VEP-Coding Acc | F1 | VEP-non-SNV Acc | F1 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| NT-500M | 60.91 | 45.20 | 67.93 | 65.97 |
| Evo2-1B | 70.07 | 49.19 | 76.17 | 66.51 |
| Qwen3-1B | 46.55 | 34.82 | 70.67 | 76.21 |
| Qwen3-4B | 48.99 | 39.58 | 61.86 | 67.60 |
| NT + Qwen3-1B | 55.58 | 54.50 | 72.82 | 76.93 |
| NT + Qwen3-4B | 60.94 | 55.66 | 65.59 | 73.00 |
| **Evo2 + Qwen3-1B** | 72.83 | 68.90 | **88.20** | **89.91** |
| **Evo2 + Qwen3-4B** | **80.21** | **80.00** | 83.85 | 85.02 |

This is the paper's cleanest evidence that the DNA channel matters: Qwen3-4B alone collapses to 48.99% on VEP-Coding (worse than random for a balanced setup), so the +31-point lift to Evo2+Qwen3-4B's 80.21% cannot be lexical leakage — gene names are present in both prompts.

### Chromatin accessibility (Appendix B, Table 3)

Enformer 17.18 → Enformer + Qwen3-4B 33.70 Macro F1 on DNase-seq. The roughly 2x lift shows the projector-into-LLM recipe is encoder-agnostic.

### Training dynamics

![GRPO reward curves](/assets/images/paper/bioreason/page_019.png)
*Figure 4: GRPO reward curves across three BioReason configurations. The 4B-backbone models converge near the theoretical max reward 2.5 by step ~400; the 1.7B model needs ~800 steps and shows higher variance. NT and Evo2 curves track each other at large LLM scale, indicating the encoder choice barely matters once the LLM is big enough.*

A telling asymmetry: on Qwen3-1B + NT, GRPO *raises* accuracy (89.31 → 91.72) but *drops* macro-F1 (81.46 → 75.06) — a textbook reward-hacking signature where the policy collapses toward majority classes. Only the 4B backbone gets monotone gains across all metrics.

## Limitations

**Authors admit.** KEGG curation introduces dataset bias and reduces coverage of poorly characterized regions; DNA truncation to 2048 tokens drops distal regulatory context; RL + long-sequence training is expensive; no robust uncertainty quantification; no multi-seed statistical tests (NeurIPS Checklist Q7 — "deferred to future work").

**Not acknowledged.**

- No ablation isolating the DNA-FM contribution from gene-symbol and pathway-topology text in the prompt — the load-bearing causal claim of the paper.
- No held-out-disease or held-out-gene KEGG split, despite the abstract claiming reasoning over "unseen biological entities."
- No faithfulness audit of the `<think>` traces — do the chains actually reflect attention over DNA embeddings, or are they stylistic LLM hallucinations grounded only in the lexical query?
- No retrieval-augmented LLM baseline (e.g., Qwen3 + KEGG pathway lookup), which is the strongest non-DNA-FM competitor.
- KEGG reasoning targets are Claude-3.7-generated; the model arguably learns to imitate Claude rather than biology.
- 290 test points across 37 diseases means rare classes have <10 items each — macro-F1 is unstable, single-seed evaluation amplifies binomial variance, and the headline 98.28% has no confidence interval.
- VEP-Coding's gnomAD-MAF>5%-benign vs. ClinVar-pathogenic setup is a known easy-discrimination split — part of the absolute performance ceiling is dataset, not model.

## Why It Matters for Medical AI

If the VEP-Coding result holds under stricter benchmarks, the recipe — *freeze a domain foundation model, project its embeddings into an LLM, supervise on chain-of-thought, refine with verifiable RL* — is a general template for grounding clinical LLMs on modalities they cannot natively read (sequencing, ECG, EEG, spectra). The interpretability surface (an explicit reasoning trace per prediction) is what makes such a system clinically auditable in principle. The medical-AI takeaway is also a methodological caution: even strong-looking benchmark wins on prompts that already encode the answer in lexical form (gene names, pathway topology) can mask the real contribution of the auxiliary modality. The community needs gene-symbol-stripped and held-out-gene evaluations before believing that DNA-FM-in-the-loop is doing the work it claims.
