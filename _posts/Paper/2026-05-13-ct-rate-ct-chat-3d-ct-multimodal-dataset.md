## TL;DR

- The authors release **CT-RATE**, the first public 3D chest CT dataset paired with free-text reports at scale: **25,692 non-contrast CTs / 50,188 reconstructions / 21,304 patients**, with Turkish-to-English translated radiology reports.
- **CT-CLIP** (CT-ViT vision encoder + CXR-Bert text encoder, symmetric InfoNCE) plus two finetuning recipes — `ClassFine` (linear probe) and `VocabFine` (open-vocab WiSE-FT variant) — and **CT-CHAT** (LLaVA-style 3D assistant on Llama 3.1, trained on 2.7 M auto-generated VQA pairs) sit on top.
- **Headline:** zero-shot CT-CLIP **beats supervised CT-Net** on all three test sets — CT-RATE internal AUROC **0.731 vs 0.629**, RAD-ChestCT **0.629 vs 0.544**, UPMC **0.567 vs 0.480** (p < 0.05 by 10,000-permutation test). CT-CHAT 8B report-generation is rated **3.44 / 4.10 / 4.52 (out of 10)** by three radiologists vs RadFM 0.55–0.91 and CT2Rep 2.52–2.74.

## Motivation

Foundation VLMs in radiology have been stuck in 2D — MIMIC-CXR, CheXpert, chest X-ray — because public 3D image-text data did not exist at the scale CLIP-style training needs. The largest prior public chest-CT resource (RAD-ChestCT, ~3,600 volumes) ships labels but no reports, so it can only support supervised learning over a fixed taxonomy. CT is the most-used cross-sectional modality, radiologists are overloaded, and self-supervised image-text pretraining is the recipe that worked in 2D — but it has been impossible to replicate without paired 3D image-text data. CT-RATE is positioned as the dataset that unblocks this; CT-CLIP and CT-CHAT are the two demonstrator foundation models on top of it.

## Core Innovation

1. **Dataset:** 25,692 non-contrast chest CTs with paired bilingual reports, plus TotalSegmentator-derived organ masks and an 18-abnormality auto-label set. Released openly on Hugging Face (`ibrahimhamamci/CT-RATE`).
2. **3D contrastive vision-language model (CT-CLIP):** CT-ViT encoder produces volume tokens; axial averaging + linear projection to a 512-d latent makes 480 × 480 × 240-voxel inputs trainable on 4× A100-80GB. CXR-Bert encodes *Findings + Impression* (ablation shows joint > findings-only > impression-only). Symmetric InfoNCE between volume and report embeddings.
3. **Open-vocab finetuning (VocabFine):** WiSE-FT-style adaptation that updates both image and text encoders, concatenating per-label positive/negative-prompt logits and aligning to binary GT via BCE. Preserves the joint embedding space so retrieval and open-vocabulary inference still work in principle (in practice, see retrieval results below).
4. **3D LLaVA assistant (CT-CHAT):** Frozen CT-CLIP vision tower → 256-query attention pooler + 2-layer MLP → Llama 3.1 (8B / 70B), Mistral 7B, or Vicuna 13B. Two-stage training (concept alignment → LoRA-128 instruction tuning on 2.7 M VQA pairs auto-generated by Llama 3.1 8B). Special tokens (`<long_answer>`, `<short_answer>`, `<report_generation>`, `<multiple_choice>`) switch task mode.

| C10 | Adding TotalSegmentator nodule structure improves report quality | Fig 6e bar bump | ⭐⭐ — only nodules, only 8B |
| C11 | Auto-labeler is high-quality (P/R ≈ 0.94–1.00) | Suppl. Table 2 on 200 held-out Turkish reports | ⭐ — **OOD accuracy on UPMC / RAD-ChestCT never measured, yet labeler defines GT for those external benchmarks** |
| C12 | Concept-alignment pretraining helps | Suppl. Table 10b | ⭐ — paper itself says "minimal impact" |
| C13 | LLM choice has minimal impact on CT-CHAT | Ext. Data Fig 6 across 4 LLMs | ⭐⭐⭐ — honest negative result |

The strongest claims (C1, C2, C8, C13) carry the headline. The weak spots cluster in one place: the **RadBERT auto-labeler is the silent backbone of every external-set GT** — its OOD accuracy is never measured, yet it produces both the supervision signal *and* the held-out labels for RAD-ChestCT and UPMC. The "zero-shot beats supervised" comparison is therefore evaluated against labels generated by the same labeler that defined CT-CLIP's training distribution.

## Method & Architecture

![CT-RATE construction and CT-CLIP contrastive pretraining](/assets/images/paper/ct-rate/fig_p010_01.png)
*Figure 1: CT-RATE construction and CT-CLIP contrastive pretraining. (a) Data acquisition pipeline; (b) RadBERT-based auto-labeler over 18 abnormalities; (c) symmetric InfoNCE training of CT-ViT + CXR-Bert; (d) volume-vs-report cosine similarity matrix; (e) zero-shot inference using positive/negative prompt pairs.*

CT volumes are resampled to 0.75 × 0.75 × 1.5 mm spacing, cropped/padded to 480 × 480 × 240 voxels, HU-clipped to [-1000, 1000] and normalized to [-1, 1]. CT-ViT applies 20 × 20 × 10-voxel patching with a two-stage spatial + causal transformer. The trick that makes the 3D budget work: encoded tokens are **averaged across the axial plane** then linearly projected to 512-d, so axial attention still propagates per-slice context but the joint-space representation stays compact. Text-side: up to 512 tokens of *Findings + Impression* into CXR-Bert, per-token 768-d outputs summed across the span, linearly projected to 512-d. Standard symmetric InfoNCE.

![Dataset demographics from CT-RATE](/assets/images/paper/ct-rate/page_003.png)
*Figure 2: CT-RATE per-abnormality abundance — class imbalance ranges from Lung nodule (~45%) down to Mosaic attenuation (~7.7%), which the paper does not reweight against during pretraining.*

![ClassFine and VocabFine finetuning](/assets/images/paper/ct-rate/fig_p012_01.png)
*Figure 3: ClassFine adds a linear probe on top of the frozen CT-CLIP encoder and is fast but loses open-vocabulary capability. VocabFine adapts WiSE-FT — for each of the 18 labels it generates a positive and negative prompt, concatenates the 36 logits, and aligns with binary GT via BCE while updating both encoders. To fit memory the 36-element logit array is split into segments of 12 per gradient step.*

For **CT-CHAT**, the frozen CT-CLIP vision tower feeds a 256-query attention-pooling projector + 2-layer GeLU MLP, then a Llama 3.1 LLM (8B trained on 2× A100, 70B on 4× A100 with DeepSpeed ZeRO-3). LoRA rank 128 / α 256 on the LLM during instruction tuning. Concept alignment freezes the LLM and trains only the projector against a fixed report-generation prompt; instruction tuning unlocks the LoRA adapters and trains on the full 2.7 M VQA mixture. A no-image system prompt forces refusal when no `<image>` is provided — a hard guardrail against the severe hallucination the authors observed in ablation.

## Experimental Results

### Multi-abnormality classification

![Main classification table across three test sets](/assets/images/paper/ct-rate/fig_p013_01.png)
*Figure 4a: Multi-abnormality classification on CT-RATE internal, RAD-ChestCT, and UPMC. CT-CLIP zero-shot beats supervised CT-Net on every metric; finetuning (ClassFine / VocabFine) gives a further small bump but loses open-vocab capability for ClassFine.*

| Set (n) | Metric | CT-Net (sup. baseline) | **CT-CLIP zero-shot** | VocabFine | ClassFine | Random |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| CT-RATE internal (1,564) | AUROC | 0.629 | **0.731** | 0.756 | 0.756 | 0.505 |
| | F1 | 0.657 | **0.707** | 0.738 | 0.724 | 0.570 |
| | Acc | 0.617 | **0.668** | 0.705 | 0.689 | 0.502 |
| RAD-ChestCT (3,630) | AUROC | 0.544 | **0.629** | 0.650 | 0.643 | 0.496 |
| | F1 | 0.564 | **0.637** | 0.677 | 0.644 | 0.555 |
| UPMC (1,024) | AUROC | 0.480 | **0.567** | 0.561 | 0.580 | 0.499 |
| | F1 | 0.613 | **0.650** | 0.636 | 0.698 | 0.565 |

All 18/18 abnormalities improve under zero-shot vs supervised on the internal set; the per-abnormality picture on UPMC is messier (some abnormalities have 0.0 precision). Significance assessed by 10,000-permutation two-sided test, p < 0.05 (Suppl. Table 5).

![Dataset-size scaling](/assets/images/paper/ct-rate/fig_p013_03.png)
*Figure 4c: Performance scales monotonically with CT-RATE training fraction. AUROC climbs from ~0.55 at 9.8% (≈ RAD-ChestCT scale) to ~0.73 at 100% — the strongest single argument for the dataset's value.*

![Prompt sweep](/assets/images/paper/ct-rate/fig_p013_02.png)
*Figure 4b: Seven-prompt sweep — variance across prompts is ~0.05 AUROC, smaller than the supervised → zero-shot gap but non-trivial. Winner: "{Abnormality} is {∅/not} present."*

### Retrieval — and the VocabFine retrieval collapse

![Retrieval results](/assets/images/paper/ct-rate/fig_p014_02.png)
*Figure 5d: Report-to-volume Recall@K. CT-CLIP zero-shot reaches Recall@100 = 0.287 (vs random 0.034), but **VocabFine collapses to ~0.020** — finetuning that "preserves the architecture" still destroys text-encoder retrieval performance. This is the clearest "no free lunch" result in the paper, and it is honestly reported.*

Volume-to-volume MAP@K on CT-RATE internal: CT-CLIP MAP@1 = 0.886 vs CT-Net 0.852, MAP@50 = 0.489 vs 0.407 (~6× over random). Volume-to-volume on RAD-ChestCT shows MAP@1 = 0.993 vs 0.989 — but the random baseline is 0.324 (only ~3× lift), reflecting heavy label skew rather than amazing model performance.

### CT-CHAT VQA + report generation

![CT-CHAT architecture, qualitative output, VQA radars, radiologist scores, clinical-efficacy bars](/assets/images/paper/ct-rate/fig_p015_01.png)
*Figure 6: CT-CHAT (a) architecture, (b) qualitative output, (c) VQA radar across long-answer / short-answer / multiple-choice / report-generation, (d) three-radiologist mean scores, (e) clinical-efficacy bars with the structured-nodule add-on.*

Three-radiologist blinded evaluation on **150 stratified cases** (50 each from low / medium / high Llama-score bands — bands defined using CT-CHAT's own Llama scores, which biases case selection toward CT-CHAT's distribution):

| Reader | **CT-CHAT** | RadFM | CT2Rep |
|---|---|---|---|
| R1 (Zurich) | **3.44** | 0.55 | 2.66 |
| R2 (Zurich) | **4.10** | 0.91 | 2.52 |
| R3 (Istanbul) | **4.52** | 0.86 | 2.74 |

CT-CHAT clears RadFM by 3–4× and CT2Rep by ~1.5–1.7× on every reader. RadFM is used at original pretrained weights (not retrained on CT-RATE) — a legitimate "off-the-shelf" framing but worth knowing. **Inter-rater agreement is not reported** (no Krippendorff α, no Cohen κ), and absolute means of 3.4–4.5 / 10 are consistent with the authors' own statement that quality "remains insufficient for clinical use."

On the VQA side, Llama-3.1 70B is marginally best — Llama Score on report generation is **5.66 (CT-CHAT 70B) vs 1.63 (CXR-LLaVA) vs 1.03 (LLaVA-Med)**; multiple-choice **8.95–9.01 vs 4.25 (LLaVA 1.6 Mistral)**. Differences across CT-CHAT's own LLM choices (Mistral 7B / Vicuna 13B / Llama 3.1 8B / 70B) are typically < 0.05 on most metrics, suggesting the lift comes from the CT-CLIP encoder + CT-RATE data, not LLM scale. The 2D-VLM comparison is **structurally unfair**: LLaVA 1.6 / LLaVA-Med / CXR-LLaVA are fed digitally reconstructed radiographs (DRRs) rather than the 3D volume CT-CHAT consumes — the paper acknowledges this and justifies via Hou et al. 2024, but it cannot be read as "specialized 3D > general 2D after controlling for input information."

## Limitations

**Authors admit:** single-center / single-country bias; ~0.05 AUROC prompt sensitivity; no 3D VLM baseline existed (so 2D VLMs are compared via DRRs); generated reports "remain insufficient for clinical use"; chest-only — no MRI / PET, no abdomen / head; no Krippendorff/Cohen agreement statistics.

**Not adequately addressed:**

- **RadBERT auto-labeler OOD accuracy is never measured**, yet it generates the GT for both RAD-ChestCT and UPMC external evaluation. Both "zero-shot beats supervised" and "generalizes across sites" headlines depend on labels produced by the same labeler that defined CT-CLIP's training distribution.
- **Radiologist study selection bias:** 150 cases stratified using CT-CHAT's own Llama scores.
- **VocabFine retrieval collapse** (0.287 → 0.020 Recall@100): "preserves open-vocab capability" is overstated — the architecture is preserved, the performance is not.
- **No multi-seed / variance reporting** for CT-CHAT training; bootstrap SD only on test-time predictions.
- **Patient-level vs reconstruction-level splitting** — split is by patient (20,000 / 1,304 patients), but 50,188 reconstructions come from 25,692 exams; whether multiple reconstructions of the *same* exam appear in both train and val is not explicitly clarified.
- **Translation noise** from Turkish → English (Google Translate API + bilingual final-year medical students) is hard to audit at 25 k report scale.
- **Carbon / compute cost** (4× A100 + 70B Llama via DeepSpeed ZeRO-3) is not discussed despite the open-foundation-model framing.

## Why It Matters for Medical AI

CT-RATE is genuinely the missing puzzle piece for 3D radiology VLMs — the open release, the bilingual reports, the 18-label auto-labeler, and the TotalSegmentator-derived organ masks are all directly reusable infrastructure. The dataset-size scaling curve in Fig 4c (monotone improvement from 9.8% → 100%) is the strongest argument that *more public 3D CT-text data* is the bottleneck, and the contrastive-pretraining recipe is what we expect from 2D radiology generalizing to 3D. Read the headline numbers, then read the auto-labeler caveat alongside them — this is a foundation dataset and a strong demonstrator, not yet a deployment-ready clinical model.
