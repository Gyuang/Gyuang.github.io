Hi, I'm Sungkyu Yang.

Integrated M.S./Ph.D. student at GIST AI Graduate School, advised by Prof. Mansu Kim. I work on multimodal alignment, language models, and spatial transcriptomics — connecting images, text, and molecular biology with modern AI.

Research Interests

  • Multimodal Alignment — learning shared representations across images, text, and structured biological data.
  • Language Models — adapting and evaluating LLMs for scientific and clinical reasoning (prompt-based, retrieval-augmented).
  • Spatial Transcriptomics — predicting gene expression from histology; bridging pathology and molecular biology with deep learning.

Adjacent interests: vision-language models for medical imaging, parameter-efficient adaptation, uncertainty quantification for trustworthy clinical AI.

What to Expect Here

The writing on this site leans toward critical paper reviews rather than summaries. Every post separates what the paper claims from the evidence that supports those claims, and frames mainstream ML work through a medical‑AI lens — especially where methods will or won't transfer from natural images to clinical modalities.

If you're a researcher, clinician, or medical‑AI practitioner looking for honest readouts of the literature, welcome.
